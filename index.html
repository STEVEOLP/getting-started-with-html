Different ways of thinking

1.Procedural thinking - Creating perfectly clear and ambiguous instructions for a computer to follow.
2.Abstract - Finding similarity or generality amongst seemingly different things. (Avoid unnecessary repitition)
3.Systems thinking - break a big problem down into smaller pieces.
4.Computer Empathy - The ability to understand what a computer is, how it works, and what it's good and bad at doing.
5.Debugging - Systematic process of relentlessly identifying the cause of a computer program that doesn't work. (Bug* = mistake somewhere in the computer code" and every programmer has a system for fixing them, they would collect evidence, generate theories, test those theories and fix the problem)

World Wide Web (A Collection of HTML Documents)

HTML = Hypertext Markup Language and it is the basis for almost every webpage.

Major Pieces:
1.HTTP: Main protocol of the web.
2.Servers: Computers that host the files that make up the web.
3.Internet: Worlds largest computer network.
4.Browser: A program that runs on your computer to display files found on the web.
